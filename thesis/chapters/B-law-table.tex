\chapter{Laws, Statuses, and Diagnostic Families}
\label{app:laws}

This appendix collects the artifact's enforced constitution \citep{lspmax}, on
which the thesis's epistemic discipline (\cref{sec:m-epistemics}) is modelled.

\section*{The non-negotiable laws}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l Y@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Law} & \textbf{Statement (paraphrased; forbidden implication prevented)} \\
\midrule
No plain base framework & the pre-fork LSP crate must not appear outside negative-control fixtures (\emph{Pass(plain LSP) $\Rightarrow$ Pass(LSP 3.18)}) \\
Maximise LSP 3.18 & every 3.18 feature row is \stat{supported-with-transcript}, \stat{refused-by-law-with-receipt}, or \stat{blocked}; never ``implied'' \\
Exact name clap-noun-verb & no invented ``CLAP'' authority (\emph{ElegantAbstraction $\Rightarrow$ ExistingAuthority}) \\
LSP is read-only & the surface emits, never mutates; mutation routes through CodeAction $\to$ receipt (\emph{observation $\Rightarrow$ mutation authority}) \\
Logs are not route proof & a logged route is not an executed route (\emph{Log(RouteIntent) $\Rightarrow$ RouteExecution}) \\
Test output is not a receipt & admission needs a bound receipt, not stdout (\emph{TestStdout $\Rightarrow$ Receipt}) \\
Tree-sitter observes, not admits & AST observation is not admission (\emph{ASTObservation $\Rightarrow$ Admission}) \\
No victory language & only bounded statuses; no ``done'', ``solved'', ``guaranteed'' \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\section*{The bounded-status vocabulary}

\admitted, \admitted-by-dogfood, \candidate, \refused, \refused-by-law-with-receipt,
\unknownstat, \stat{unsupported}, \partialstat, \stat{regression-risk}, \openstat,
\stat{failset-nonempty}, \stat{matrix-incomplete}, \stat{supported-with-transcript},
\blocked. Three of these---\admitted, \refused, \unknownstat---are kept mutually
disjoint and never collapsed (\cref{thm:noncollapse}).

\section*{Diagnostic families of the anti-cheat canary}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l Y@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Family} & \textbf{Detects} \\
\midrule
\code{ANTI-LLM-SURFACE-*} & fake protocol or dependency surface \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-AUTH-*} & fake authority or abstraction \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-RECEIPT-*} & fake or malformed receipts \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-ROUTE-*} & fake routes (logs in place of route evidence) \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-MUT-*} & mutation bypassing the actuation chain \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-TEST-*} & test laundering \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-VERSION-*} & CalVer / version-law violations \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-CLAIM-*} & victory or status overclaim \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-OCEL-*} & a diagnostic emitted without its object-centric event \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-DECLARE-*} & violations of the Declare-encoded laws \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-ORACLE-*} & Oracle classes A8--A12 (\cref{tab:oracle}) \\
\code{ANTI-LLM-HOLLOW-*} & hollow / placeholder implementations \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\noindent The \code{DECLARE} and \code{ORACLE} families are the artifact's literal
use of van der Aalst's formalisms---declarative constraints and object-centric
conformance---to police its own conduct (\cref{sec:art-ocel}).
